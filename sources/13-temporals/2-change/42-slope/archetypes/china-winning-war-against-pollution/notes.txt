In 2014, China declared a “war on pollution”. It was an important shift for the Chinese government. No longer prioritising economic growth at all costs. Admitting the scale of the problem for the first time.

New policies include reducing dependence on coal, cutting vehicle emissions, investing in renewables and reporting air quality statistics to the public. It’s been a remarkable success – particularly in the over-populated mega cities. If these trajectories continue, many lives will be saved.
